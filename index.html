How This AI Works

1. The Neural Network (The Brain)

Each bird has its own unique "brain" - a simple neural network. It makes decisions based on 4 inputs:

  • Bird Y Position: Vertical position on screen.
  • Pipe Distance: Horizontal distance to the next pipe.
  • Pipe Gap Height: Vertical position of the safe gap.
  • Velocity: Current vertical speed of the bird.

These inputs flow through a Hidden Layer (where the "thinking" happens) and result in a single Output. If the output is > 0.5, the bird jumps!

2. Genetic Algorithm (Evolution)

At the start, 50 birds are spawned with completely random brains. Most will fail immediately. This is "Generation 1".

When all birds die, the next generation is created using Darwinian Evolution:

  • Selection: Birds that flew further (higher fitness) are more likely to be picked as parents.
  • Crossover: (Simplified here) We copy the best bird's brain.
  • Mutation: We slightly tweak the neural connections (weights) of the offspring. This introduces new behaviors.
